Here are 5 best 'Formula Structures' for Pinterest titles, based on the provided data, along with their logic:

1.  **[Number] + [Adjective] + [Keyword 1] + [Keyword 2] + [Benefit/Outcome]**
    *   **Logic:** This formula is highly effective because it immediately grabs attention with a specific number (suggesting a curated list or collection), uses an appealing adjective to evoke positive emotion, and clearly states the core topic ("Keyword 1," "Keyword 2"). The inclusion of a "Benefit/Outcome" tells users *why* they should care – what problem it solves or what desirable result it offers. This structure is prevalent in the data, indicating its strong performance in attracting clicks by promising actionable advice and desirable results.

2.  **[Number] + [Keyword 1] + [Benefit/Outcome] + for [Target Audience/Specific Application]**
    *   **Logic:** This structure excels by prioritizing the desired outcome or solution ("Benefit/Outcome") upfront, immediately resonating with users' needs. The "Number" provides structure, and the "Keyword 1" ensures relevance. Specifying the "Target Audience/Specific Application" makes the content feel tailored and directly applicable to the user's situation, increasing its perceived value and likelihood of being clicked.

3.  **DIY + [Keyword 1] + for [Benefit/Outcome]**
    *   **Logic:** This is a classic and highly successful DIY formula. The "DIY" immediately signals hands-on, achievable projects, which is a core appeal of Pinterest. "Keyword 1" identifies the subject matter, and "Benefit/Outcome" highlights the value proposition – what the user will gain by undertaking the project. This structure appeals to users looking for practical, cost-effective, and customizable solutions.

4.  **[Keyword 1] + [Keyword 2] + Ideas to [Benefit/Outcome]**
    *   **Logic:** This formula is straightforward and highly effective for searchability. It clearly defines the topic with two keywords, making it easy for users to find what they're looking for. The "Ideas" part suggests a collection or inspiration, and the "Benefit/Outcome" explains the purpose or advantage of these ideas, providing a compelling reason to click. It's a versatile structure that covers a broad range of topics.

5.  **[Adjective] + [Keyword 1] + [Keyword 2] + [Benefit/Outcome]**
    *   **Logic:** This structure relies on strong descriptive language to draw users in. The "Adjective" creates an immediate emotional connection or sets an expectation (e.g., "Gorgeous," "Stunning," "Easy," "Cheap"). "Keyword 1" and "Keyword 2" establish the topic, and the "Benefit/Outcome" provides the crucial "why click" factor. It’s effective because it combines emotional appeal with practical information and a clear promise of value.
